Five Key Definitions for Reading Music
1. Music Notes: are sounds which are represented by seven letters, A through G. B is a higher frequency note than A, C is an higher frequency note than B, and so on. The next full note above a G is an A.

2. The Stave: Is the set of five horizontal lines on which music notes appear.

2. The Clef: is the first symbol that appears on the stave. It is the reference point which tells the reader how to read the notes on the stave.
3. Middle C: is the C near the middle of the piano keyboard. If most of the notes written on a stave are above middle C, the stave will have a treble clef. Otherwise, if most of the notes on the stave fall below middle C, the stave will have a bass clef.
The Treble Clef: is a cursive G symbol that appears on the second line from the bottom of a stave. The symbol indicates that the notes that appear on that line is the G above middle C. All other notes on the stave can be deteremined by looking at their location relative to the treble clef. For the example, the note above the treble clef is an A and the note beow the treble clef is an F.
The Bass Clef: is a music symbol that appears on the second line from the top of a stave. The symbol indicates that notes on that line are the F below middle C. All other notes on the stave can be deteremined by looking at their location relative to the bass clef. i.e. the note above the treble clef is an G and the note beow the treble clef is an E.
